# My story

- I hold a **Computer Engineering** degree from **ITA** (Instituto Tecnológico de Aeronáutica), one of Brazil's top engineering schools 🇧🇷 (2010–2015).
- In 2013, I spent a year as an exchange student in The Netherlands 🇳🇱, studying at both Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e) and the University of Twente (UT).
- I joined **Google** as an intern in 2015 and returned as a **full-time software engineer** from **2016 to 2020**.
  - **Internship:** Rewrote the automated testing infrastructure for an iOS app using Objective-C, Python, Bazel, and EarlGrey.
  - **Full-time:** Worked heavily with C++, Python, Java, JavaScript, and inevitably, some Shell.
- After 2020, I worked at two startups—primarily using TypeScript, React, and Node.js—before **deciding to build my own products as an Indie Founder**.

This is the short version. For more details, check out [my website](https://marcelo.land) or my [LinkedIn profile](https://linkedin.com/in/marcelocra).

## At Google

Google is an incredible environment that deeply values software engineering. Best practices like rigorous code reviews, testing, and documentation are embedded in the daily culture, ensuring high code quality and maintainability.

I learned a massive amount by building software at scale alongside brilliant people. Ultimately, though, the itch to build my own company won out.

## After quitting

Post-Google, I joined a startup as a technical Product Manager to learn the business side by working closely with the CEO. Then, I dove into digital marketing and online ads to understand how to successfully bring products to market.

Later, I got the rare opportunity to merge these two worlds as a developer in a marketing startup. I leveraged my unique domain knowledge to architect their new system and database.

Finally, I transitioned into a Full Stack Product Engineer and Indie Founder, combining my technical depth and marketing experience to build products I felt were missing from the market.
